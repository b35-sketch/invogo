Privacy Policy for InvoGo

This Privacy Policy explains how InvoGo handles information when you use the app. InvoGo is provided by Cooba Dev.

Provider: Cooba Dev
Contact: coob-dev@outlook.com
Last updated: 23 April 2026

1. Who we are

InvoGo is an invoice creation and document export app provided by Cooba Dev (“we”, “us”, “our”). This Privacy Policy describes how information is processed when you use InvoGo.

2. Scope of this Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy applies to the InvoGo mobile app and any static privacy page published for the app, including a GitHub-hosted version of this policy.

3. How InvoGo works

InvoGo is designed to work primarily on your device. Based on the app’s current implementation, information you enter into the app is generally stored locally on your device or in your browser storage, rather than being uploaded to a dedicated account-based cloud service operated by us.

4. Information processed by the app

Depending on how you use InvoGo, the app may process information such as:

  • your business or trading name;
  • your contact details, address details, bank details, and invoice settings;
  • client names and client address information that you enter;
  • invoice details such as invoice numbers, job descriptions, dates, prices, tax settings, and totals;
  • generated invoice previews, PDFs, or other exported files you choose to create;
  • preferences and settings used to customise invoices and app behaviour.

5. Where your information is stored

InvoGo currently stores user-entered invoice and settings data locally on your device or in your browser storage. We do not currently provide an account system or an always-on remote syncing service for this data.

This means the information you enter typically remains under your control on the device you use, unless you choose to export, share, email, print, back up, or otherwise send it elsewhere.

6. When information may leave your device

Your information may leave your device only when you take an action such as:

  • exporting or saving an invoice or PDF file;
  • sharing a file using your device’s share features;
  • emailing an invoice or document to someone;
  • printing a document through a connected service or device;
  • backing up your device or browser data using third-party tools or platforms.

If you use third-party apps, email providers, printers, cloud storage services, or operating system sharing tools, those third parties may process your data under their own privacy policies and terms.

7. Information we do not currently collect through a dedicated backend

Based on the current version of InvoGo, we do not currently operate a dedicated backend account system that routinely collects and stores your invoice content on our servers for syncing or account management.

We also do not currently require you to create a user account to use the core app functions.

8. Device permissions and platform features

InvoGo may rely on device features or permissions where relevant, for example to access files, save documents, share exports, or work with images you choose to use. Any such access is used to provide the feature you request.

Permission prompts are controlled by your device operating system, and you can usually manage them in your device settings.

9. Legal basis for processing

Where UK data protection law applies, our basis for processing is generally that:

  • processing is necessary to provide the app functions you request;
  • processing is necessary for our legitimate interests in operating, maintaining, and improving the app; and
  • in some cases, processing is based on your consent, such as where your device requests permission for access to certain features.

10. Data retention

Because InvoGo is local-first, data you enter may remain stored on your device or in your browser storage until you delete it, clear app/browser data, uninstall the app, reset your device, or overwrite the stored information.

Exported files, emailed invoices, printed copies, or backups may remain available in the places where you save or send them, subject to the settings and policies of those services or devices.

11. Data security

We aim to design the app so that information is handled with reasonable care. However, no method of electronic storage, transmission, or device usage can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

You are responsible for securing your own device, email account, backups, exported files, and any third-party services you use in connection with InvoGo.

12. Your responsibilities

You are responsible for reviewing the information you enter into InvoGo, including invoice content, client details, tax settings, payment details, and exported documents, and for ensuring that your use of the app complies with applicable law.

13. Children’s privacy

InvoGo is not directed at children. We do not knowingly seek to collect personal data from children through a dedicated account system in the current version of the app. If you believe a child has provided personal information in connection with the app, please contact us at coob-dev@outlook.com.

14. Your rights

Depending on your circumstances and applicable law, you may have rights relating to personal data, including rights to access, correct, erase, restrict, or object to certain processing.

Because InvoGo currently stores most entered data locally on your own device, you can often exercise practical control directly by editing or deleting that data in the app, deleting exported files, or removing app/browser storage from your device.

If you contact us about privacy matters, we will do our best to respond in line with applicable legal requirements.

15. Third-party services

If you choose to use third-party email apps, storage providers, printer services, file-sharing tools, app stores, or device backup services in connection with InvoGo, your information may also be handled by those third parties. Their practices are governed by their own terms and privacy policies, not this Privacy Policy.

16. International transfers

If you email, share, store, back up, or otherwise transmit files or data using third-party services, your information may be transferred outside the UK depending on the providers you choose to use.

17. Changes to this Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes to the app, legal requirements, or operational needs. The updated version will be posted at the relevant public policy URL and will show the latest “Last updated” date.
